---
title: Choking adult (conscious)
severity: urgent
setting: any
danger: Stand to the side and slightly behind the casualty. Be prepared to support them if they collapse.
response:
  - "Ask 'Are you choking?' — if they can speak or cough effectively, encourage coughing."
  - "If cough is ineffective or silent: give 5 sharp back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand."
  - "Check the mouth between attempts — only remove visible obstructions, no finger sweeps."
  - "If still obstructed: 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich) — fist above navel, pull sharply inwards and upwards."
  - "Alternate 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until the obstruction clears or the casualty becomes unconscious."
  - "If they collapse: lower them to the ground, call 112, begin CPR. Compressions can dislodge the obstruction."
skills:
  - Back blows
  - Abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre)
  - Transition to CPR
callout: After any successful abdominal thrust, the casualty needs medical assessment — internal injury is possible.
---
