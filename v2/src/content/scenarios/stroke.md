---
title: Suspected stroke (FAST positive)
severity: critical
setting: any
danger: Casualty may collapse — be ready to support them. Avoid feeding them anything.
response:
  - "Run FAST assessment — even one positive sign means call 112 immediately."
  - "Face: ask them to smile. Is one side drooping?"
  - "Arms: ask them to raise both arms. Does one drift down?"
  - "Speech: ask them to repeat a simple phrase. Is it slurred or strange?"
  - "Time: note the exact time symptoms started — this is critical for the thrombolysis window."
  - "Call 112 and say 'suspected stroke'. Give them the time of onset."
  - "Sit or lie the casualty in a comfortable position, head and shoulders slightly raised."
  - "Nothing by mouth — no food, water, or medication. Swallow may be impaired."
  - "Monitor airway and breathing. Reassure them."
skills:
  - FAST assessment
  - Time-of-onset recording
  - Airway monitoring
callout: Thrombolysis treatment works within roughly 4.5 hours of onset. Time is brain — minutes matter.
---
