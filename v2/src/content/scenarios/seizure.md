---
title: Tonic-clonic seizure
severity: urgent
setting: any
danger: Clear hard or sharp objects from the area. Move bystanders back. Cushion the head with something soft if you can.
response:
  - "Do NOT restrain the casualty. Do NOT put anything in their mouth — including your fingers."
  - "Note the time the seizure started."
  - "Protect them from injury while it runs its course."
  - "Once the jerking stops, gently turn them onto their side (recovery position)."
  - "Open the airway, monitor breathing, stay with them as they come around."
  - "Reassure quietly — they may be confused for several minutes (post-ictal phase)."
  - "Call 112 if: first seizure, lasts over 5 minutes, repeats without recovery, injury occurred, pregnancy, or not waking up."
skills:
  - Scene management
  - Recovery position
  - Timing the seizure
callout: Most seizures end on their own within 2–3 minutes. The danger is usually injury during, or airway after.
---
