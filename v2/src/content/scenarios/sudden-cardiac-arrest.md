---
title: Sudden cardiac arrest (adult)
severity: critical
setting: any
danger: Scene safe? Check for hazards before approaching — moving vehicles, water, live electrics.
response:
  - "Check responsiveness: tap shoulders, shout 'Are you OK?' — no response."
  - "Open the airway: head-tilt, chin-lift."
  - "Look, listen, feel for normal breathing for 10 seconds. Agonal gasps don't count."
  - "Call 112. Send a bystander for the nearest AED."
  - "Start CPR: 30 chest compressions, depth 5–6 cm, rate 100–120/min."
  - "Give 2 rescue breaths after each set of 30 compressions."
  - "As soon as AED arrives, switch it on and follow the voice prompts. Continue CPR while pads are placed."
  - "Continue 30:2 cycles until EMS take over, AED advises stop, or casualty shows signs of life."
skills:
  - CPR (30:2)
  - AED operation
  - Recognising agonal gasps
callout: Agonal gasps look like breathing but are not. If breaths are irregular and infrequent, treat as not breathing.
---
