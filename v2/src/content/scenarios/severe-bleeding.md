---
title: Severe external bleeding
severity: critical
setting: any
danger: Put on gloves if available. Avoid direct blood contact. Move sharp objects out of the way.
response:
  - "Apply firm, direct pressure over the wound with the cleanest cloth or dressing available."
  - "Maintain pressure — do NOT lift the dressing to check progress."
  - "If blood soaks through, add another dressing on top and keep pressing."
  - "Lay the casualty down and raise the bleeding limb if no fracture is suspected."
  - "Call 112."
  - "If catastrophic limb haemorrhage and direct pressure fails: apply a tourniquet above the wound, tighten until bleeding stops, write the application time on it."
  - "Treat for shock: keep the casualty warm, reassure, monitor breathing."
skills:
  - Direct pressure haemostasis
  - Tourniquet application (catastrophic limb bleed)
  - Shock management
callout: Tourniquets are last-line — only for limb bleeds where pressure has failed or you cannot maintain it. Never on neck or torso.
---
