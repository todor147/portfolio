---
title: Hypothermia (cold-water immersion)
severity: urgent
setting: beach
danger: Get the casualty out of the wind and rain. Handle gently — rough movement can trigger arrhythmia.
response:
  - "Remove from the water with the body kept as horizontal as possible — sudden upright posture can cause cardiovascular collapse."
  - "Replace wet clothing with dry blankets. Cover the head."
  - "Insulate the casualty from the ground — they will keep losing heat through it."
  - "Do NOT rub limbs, give alcohol, or apply direct heat to the trunk."
  - "Allow only small sips of warm sweet liquids if the casualty is fully alert."
  - "Call 112. Treat for shock."
  - "If unresponsive and not breathing normally: start CPR — keep going. Hypothermic casualties can recover from extended CPR; do not stop until rewarmed and assessed."
skills:
  - Passive rewarming
  - Gentle handling
  - Extended CPR for hypothermic arrest
callout: "Old saying: nobody is dead until they are warm and dead. CPR continues much longer than usual."
---
