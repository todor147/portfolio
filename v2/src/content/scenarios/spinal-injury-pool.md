---
title: Suspected spinal injury (pool diving)
severity: critical
setting: pool
danger: Stop other swimmers from entering the water near the casualty. Clear the immediate area.
response:
  - "Do NOT move the casualty out of the water unless their airway is at risk."
  - "Apply an in-water spinal hold: hands gently support the head with thumbs at the cheeks, keeping head, neck and spine in a single line."
  - "Float the casualty to the side of the pool, maintaining alignment, head facing up."
  - "Call 112. Send a colleague for the backboard."
  - "Brief a second rescuer to take over the head-hold while you prepare the board."
  - "Extract using a backboard with at least three rescuers — one always controls the head."
  - "Monitor breathing. If they stop breathing, you must prioritise airway over spinal precaution."
skills:
  - In-water spinal stabilisation
  - C-spine control
  - Backboard extraction
callout: Spinal precautions never override basic life support. A breathing casualty stays in the water; a non-breathing casualty comes out.
---
