---
title: Drowning casualty (recently removed from water)
severity: critical
setting: pool / beach
danger: Get the casualty out of the water safely first. Avoid direct contact rescue if you are not trained — throw or reach.
response:
  - "Once on a firm flat surface, check responsiveness."
  - "Open the airway and check breathing for 10 seconds."
  - "If not breathing normally: give 5 initial rescue breaths (this differs from standard CPR — hypoxia is the primary problem in drowning)."
  - "Call 112. Send a bystander for the AED."
  - "Begin standard CPR cycles: 30 compressions to 2 breaths."
  - "Use AED as soon as available — dry the casualty's chest before placing pads."
  - "Continue until EMS take over or the casualty starts breathing normally."
skills:
  - Modified CPR (5 initial breaths)
  - In-water rescue (throw / reach / wade)
  - AED on wet casualty
callout: The 5 initial rescue breaths before compressions is a drowning-specific deviation taught in CFR-A. Hypoxia preceded the cardiac arrest.
---
