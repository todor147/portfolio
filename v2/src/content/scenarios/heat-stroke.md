---
title: Heat exhaustion progressing to heat stroke
severity: urgent
setting: beach
danger: Get the casualty out of direct sun. Loosen tight clothing.
response:
  - "Move to a cool, shaded place. Lie them down with legs slightly raised."
  - "Cool actively: wet the skin with cool water, fan them, place ice packs at the neck, armpits and groin."
  - "Loosen or remove excess clothing."
  - "If fully conscious and able to swallow, offer small sips of cool water."
  - "Monitor for warning signs of heat stroke: altered mental state, confusion, vomiting, very hot dry skin, body temperature over 40°C."
  - "If any heat stroke signs appear, call 112 immediately — this is a medical emergency."
  - "Continue active cooling until EMS arrive or temperature drops below 39°C."
  - "If they become unresponsive, place in the recovery position and monitor breathing."
skills:
  - Active cooling
  - Heat stroke recognition
  - Hydration management
callout: Heat exhaustion is recoverable with cooling and rest. Heat stroke (altered mental state, hot dry skin) is life-threatening.
---
